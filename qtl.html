QTL Mapping

  

Interval Mapping

The estimated threshold for both methods (EM and HK) were similar (Figure 1.A and Figure 1.B). This similarity conducted to an equal QTL detection, on what, 3 QTLs was found for the trait analyzed (Figure 1.C and Figure 1.D). Expectation Maximization and Halley-Knot presented similar results (Figure 1.E) Those QTLs were found in chromosome 6, 13 and 14 (Figure 1.F). The position and LOD of these are shown in Table 1.

Composed Interval Mapping

Multiple Interval Mapping

Genetic map with identified QTL are presented in Figure 4.A. Using MIM, we also detected 3 QTL, but one of them was different . QTLs in the linkage group 6 and 13 were identified, as in IM and CIM, and a new one was found in linkage group 8 (Figure 4.B). This new QTL had smaller effect compared to the other (Table 3). A great advantage in the use of MIM is the possibility to verify epistatic interactions between QLTs. Therefore, we can check that QTL in chromosome 6 and 13 interact (Figure das bolinhas ligadas), what may explain the detection of both in all QTL methods used. Comparing the LOD score curve of CIM and MIM, it is possible to confirm that they detected QTL 6 and 13 in the same position, once the peak of curves was too close (Figure 4.D). Peak on chromosome 8 for CIM was also identified, but not enough to cross the permutation threshhold.
